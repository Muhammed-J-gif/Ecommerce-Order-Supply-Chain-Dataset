# 📊 KPI Definitions & Calculations

This document outlines the key performance indicators (KPIs) and analytical dimensions tracked within this project. These metrics support visibility into revenue performance, customer behavior, operational efficiency, and fulfillment quality.

---

## 🧾 Revenue & Order Metrics

### **Total Revenue**
The sum of all completed order values within the analysis period.  
Useful for understanding overall business performance and growth trends.

### **Average Order Value (AOV)**
AOV = Total Revenue / Number of Orders
Indicates customer spending behavior and helps evaluate pricing and upsell opoortunities.

### **Daily Order Volume**
The count of orders placed each day.  
Supports demand forecasting, staffing decisions, and anomaly detection.

---

## 🛍️ Product & Category Insights

### **Category‑Level Sales**
Breakdown of revenue and order volume by product category.  
This can help identify top‑performing categories and areas needing optimization.

---

## 👤 Customer Metrics

### **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)**
An estimate of the total revenue expected from a customer over their relationship with the business.  
Useful for segmentation, retention strategies, and marketing ROI analysis.

---

## 🚚 Fulfillment & Delivery KPIs

### **On‑Time Delivery Rate**

On‑Time Delivery Rate = Orders Delivered On Time / Total Delivered Orders

Measures fulfillment reliability and carrier performance.

### **Carrier Performance Metrics**
Evaluation of shipping carriers based on:
- Delivery speed  
- On‑time percentage  
- Delivery exceptions  
- Cost efficiency  

---

## 💳 Payment & Checkout Metrics

### **Payment Success Rate**

Payment Success Rate = Successful Payments/Total Payment Attempts

Highlights checkout friction, payment gateway issues, or fraud‑related declines.

---

## 🏭 Operational KPIs (External Data Required)

Some operational metrics depend on systems outside the provided dataset. These may include:

- **Warehouse backlog** (pending orders not yet processed)  
- **Pick/pack cycle time**  
- **Inventory accuracy**  
- **Fulfillment SLA adherence**  

If needed, these can be integrated later through additional data sources.

---

## 📌 Notes
- All KPIs should be calculated consistently across reporting periods.  
- Any assumptions or data transformations must be documented in the analysis code or notebooks.  
- External data dependencies should be flagged early to avoid gaps in reporting.
